Poorly Accessible Subway Problem in the Tokyo City.
Due to serious aging issues, Japanese cities are gradually improving accessibility. Tokyo, the capital of Japan, has achieved a systematic level of accessibility throughout the city, including airports, subway, and various infrastructures. However, through our real-life experiences and past research, we found that Tokyo’s subway still has an accessibility problem in many important transportation hubs that have many elderly population in the centre area of Tokyo.



How is the Situation of Subway in the Six Central Wards of Tokyo?

The map to the right shows all of the subway stations in the six central wards of Tokyo, highlighted in pink.


In our project, we try to assess the degree of subway station accessibility through figuring out the proportion of accessible entrances. On clicking, you will see that most of the stations are not accessible as we thought. Users can find out  from here which entrances of the subway station they are looking for are barrier-free (not visible on Google Maps)

50 % of Subway Stations are Low-Accessible.
In our project, we try to assess the degree of accessibility of the subway stations through classifying the proportion of accessible entrances.
0-30%: Low accessible
30%-70%: Middle accessible
>70%: Accessible
You'll notice that half of the stations are low-accessible.


Purpose:
Our project seeks to help local governments take action to improve accessibility by presenting and engaging users through an interactive website to determine which stations are accessible, which are in need of renovation, as well as these stations map onto populations which likely need access to subway stations the most. Also, they can give us some feedback if they face some non-accessible subway stations.
